Vegam Solutions is a United States-headquartered industrial software company founded in 2000 by Subramanyam Kasibhat and Savita Kasibhat. The company develops software for manufacturing operations management and industrial automation.

The company originally operated as Aureole Technologies Private Limited in Bengaluru, India, before establishing a Singapore entity in 2015. The name "Vegam" (pronounced [ʋeː.ɡɐ̃]; VAY-gum) is derived from the Sanskrit word वेगं, meaning "speed" or "velocity".

Vegam Solutions develops software systems used in manufacturing execution, production management, industrial IoT integration, and factory operations across process and discrete industries. According to company materials, its software deployments have been used across more than 300 manufacturing plants in approximately 60 countries.

The company's products include AI Intime, an enterprise AI platform; Vegam Robotics, a robotics and industrial automation initiative; and Vegam SFS, a manufacturing operations management platform.

Publicly reported deployments have included projects involving companies such as Henkel, MBCC Group, and BASF.

History [edit]

Founding (2000–2010)

Edit

The company was founded in January 2000 by Subramanyam Kasibhat and Savita Kasibhat, and operated as Aureole Technologies Private Limited, based in Bengaluru, India.[12] Its earliest work was as a software and hardware solutions provider for manufacturing customers, including products marketed at the time as i-PAS, an SAP mobility enabler for manufacturing plants deployed in Australia, China, and Malaysia, and an early manufacturing execution system used to rank factory performance.[12]

According to the company's founder, the business spent approximately seven years working closely with a single early customer — described in independent reporting as a German manufacturer — before rewriting the software into a multi-tenant product offering.[13]

Transition to a product company (2011–2019)

Edit

During this period, the company shifted its focus from services delivery to platform development.[12] In August 2015, a Singapore-incorporated entity, Vegam Solutions Pte Ltd, was established, and the company began marketing itself as an Industry 4.0 enabler offering platforms and modules for smart factories.[12] The Bengaluru entity, Aureole Technologies Private Limited, continued to operate alongside the Singapore parent until September 2022, when it was rebranded under the Vegam Solutions name.[12]

The company has remained privately held and bootstrapped throughout this period.[13]

Multinational deployments (2020–2024)

Edit

From 2020, Vegam's engagement with Henkel Adhesive Technologies — which operates more than 130 manufacturing sites worldwide — was the subject of features in Manufacturing Digital, Technology Magazine, and Business Chief UK & Europe.[2][3][6] In a December 2020 profile of Henkel Adhesive Technologies, the company's then–Head of Digital Operations, Dr Nick Miesen, characterised the Vegam system as a powerful tool from the shop floor to the board room.[7]

In early 2022, Vegam was selected by the MBCC Group — a construction-chemicals manufacturer subsequently acquired by Sika AG — to deploy its overall equipment effectiveness module across more than twenty production lines internationally, on a software-as-a-service commercial model. The engagement was reported to have reached commercial amortisation in under twelve months.[4]

Corporate restructuring and GenAI evolution (2024–2025)

Edit

In April 2024, a related Indian operating entity, Vegam Smart Factory Solutions Private Limited, was registered in Bangalore.[14] As part of a broader move into generative-AI–driven manufacturing intelligence, the company launched AI Intime in January 2025, an enterprise agentic-AI platform extending the group's focus beyond conventional manufacturing-operations software.[citation needed] The relationship between this 2024 Indian entity, the Singapore-incorporated Vegam Solutions Pte Ltd, and any US-incorporated affiliate is not visible in the publicly accessible filings reviewed for this article. Products [edit]

Edit

AI Intime

AI Intime is an enterprise artificial-intelligence platform developed by Vegam Solutions for manufacturing and industrial operations environments. The platform supports role-specific AI agents designed for operational workflows, industrial data access, and enterprise knowledge retrieval. [15]

The platform has been used in manufacturing-related use cases including analytical laboratory report analysis, operational KPI retrieval, customer-support workflows, industrial training systems, and voice-enabled shop-floor assistance. [15]

According to company materials, AI Intime supports integrations with enterprise systems including manufacturing execution systems (MES), enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems, laboratory information-management systems (LIMS), and industrial operational databases. [15]

Vegam Robotics

Edit

Vegam Robotics

Vegam Robotics is the robotics and industrial-automation division of Vegam Solutions focused on autonomous material movement, industrial sampling systems, and factory automation technologies. The division develops robotics systems intended for manufacturing and industrial operations environments. [16]

Products developed under the Vegam Robotics initiative have included AutoDock, an autonomous docking and charging system for industrial vehicles; SampleRun, an automated industrial sample-handling system; and autonomous mobile robots (AMRs) designed for material movement and logistics workflows within manufacturing facilities. [16]

According to company materials, the robotics platform has been designed for use in industrial environments including manufacturing plants, warehouses, and process-industry operations. [16]

Vegam SFS

Edit

Vegam SFS

Vegam SFS is a manufacturing operations management (MOM) and manufacturing execution system (MES) platform developed by Vegam Solutions for process and discrete manufacturing industries. The platform is designed to manage production workflows, industrial traceability, material movement, quality operations, and plant-level execution activities across manufacturing environments. [17]

Development of the platform began during the company's early manufacturing digitisation projects in the late 2000s, including an initial deployment associated with a Henkel manufacturing facility in Australia in 2007. According to company materials, the platform has subsequently been deployed across manufacturing environments involving more than 300 plants in approximately 60 countries. [17]

Vegam SFS integrates with enterprise and industrial systems including enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems, distributed control systems (DCS), programmable logic controllers (PLC), supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA) systems, laboratory information-management systems (LIMS), and industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) infrastructure. [17]

The platform includes modules related to goods receipt (GR), warehouse and staging operations, manufacturing execution, dispatch management, quality management, maintenance management, production scheduling, industrial traceability, operational efficiency monitoring, industrial labelling, and predictive-maintenance workflows. [17]

According to company materials, the platform was developed to support paperless manufacturing operations through digital workflows, electronic batch records, digital standard operating procedures (SOPs), real-time production monitoring, and plant-wide operational traceability. [17]

Modules

Vegam SFS includes operational modules covering goods receipt (GR), warehouse and staging operations, manufacturing execution, dispatch and logistics workflows, laboratory and quality-management operations, predictive maintenance, operational-efficiency monitoring, industrial labelling, and production scheduling. [17]

  • Goods receipt (GR) – manages inbound material handling, unloading validation, weighbridge integration, and inventory registration.
  • Staging – supports material preparation, warehouse staging, pre-weighing, and production-line material movement.
  • Manufacturing – manages production execution workflows, digital SOPs, process traceability, and operator guidance.
  • Dispatch – supports outbound logistics, shipment preparation, packaging validation, and material traceability.
  • LIMS and quality management – integrates laboratory operations, quality checkpoints, certificate-of-analysis workflows, and testing processes.
  • Predictive maintenance – includes maintenance scheduling, equipment-health monitoring, and industrial condition-monitoring workflows.
  • Operational efficiency – supports production KPI monitoring, downtime analysis, and overall equipment effectiveness (OEE) tracking.
  • Industrial labelling – manages production and regulatory labelling workflows, including GHS-compliant labels.

Legacy products

Legacy products

Prior to the expansion of the Vegam SFS platform, the company developed several software products related to manufacturing operations, industrial monitoring, maintenance management, and production scheduling.

Earlier products that were later retired or consolidated into broader manufacturing operations offerings included SFS Lite; vCMS, a condition-monitoring system for industrial equipment; Vegam 4i; Vegam Label; vMaint4, a maintenance-management platform supporting preventive and predictive maintenance workflows; vOEE, an overall equipment effectiveness (OEE) monitoring system; vProMax; and Zing.[2][3][4]

Several earlier products were later consolidated into broader manufacturing operations and industrial automation offerings developed by the company.

Operations [edit]

Vegam Solutions is headquartered in Singapore, and operates in India, Singapore, the United Arab Emirates, and the United States.[citation needed] The company's principal engineering and delivery centres in India are in Bengaluru and Hubli; the registered office of its Indian operating entity is at Spatium South Park, JP Nagar 3rd Phase, Bengaluru.[14] As of 2025, the company reported approximately 180 employees, primarily based in India, and was generating annual revenue in the region of US$10 million; it has remained bootstrapped, with no outside venture funding beyond a 2011 seed round.[13]

Corporate affairs [edit]

Leadership

Subramanyam Kasibhat is the chief executive officer and co-founder of Vegam Solutions. Prior to founding the company, he worked at Texas Instruments India in engineering and business-development roles. [11]

Savita Kasibhat is the chief technology officer and co-founder of the company and has been associated with its engineering and technology-development activities since its founding. [11]

Other senior leadership associated with the company has included Hemang Shah, Vice President and Head of IIoT Products and Solutions Management, and Anant Kolhar, Vice President of Strategy and Operations Management. [11]

Ownership and funding

Vegam is privately held and has remained bootstrapped, without external venture funding.[13]
