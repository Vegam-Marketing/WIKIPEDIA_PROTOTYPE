Vegam Solutions is a United States-headquartered industrial software company founded in 2000 by Subramanyam Kasibhat and Savita Kasibhat. The company develops software for manufacturing operations management and industrial automation.

The company originally operated as Aureole Technologies Private Limited in Bengaluru, India, before establishing a Singapore entity in 2015. The name "Vegam" (pronounced [ʋeː.ɡɐ̃]; VAY-gum) is derived from the Sanskrit word वेगं, meaning "speed" or "velocity".

Vegam Solutions develops software systems used in manufacturing execution, production management, industrial IoT integration, and factory operations across process and discrete industries. According to company materials, its software deployments have been used across more than 300 manufacturing plants in approximately 60 countries.

The company's products include AI Intime, an enterprise AI platform; Vegam Robotics, a robotics and industrial automation initiative; and Vegam SFS, a manufacturing operations management platform.

Publicly reported deployments have included projects involving companies such as Henkel, MBCC Group, and BASF.

History [edit]

Founding and early operations (2000–2010)

Vegam Solutions was founded in January 2000 by Subramanyam Kasibhat and Savita Kasibhat, and originally operated as Aureole Technologies Private Limited in Bengaluru, India. [5]

The company's early activities focused on software and hardware systems for manufacturing operations, including industrial mobility applications and manufacturing-execution solutions for process-manufacturing environments. Early deployments included manufacturing facilities in Australia, China, and Malaysia. [1]

According to statements by the company's founder in later interviews, the business spent several years working closely with an early manufacturing customer before evolving its software into a broader multi-tenant manufacturing-operations platform. [5]

In 2007, the company completed an early paperless-manufacturing deployment associated with a manufacturing facility in Australia. [17]

Transition to manufacturing operations platforms (2010–2024)

During the 2010s, the company expanded from project-based manufacturing software deployments into broader manufacturing operations management and industrial digitisation platforms. [1] [2]

In 2015, the company established a Singapore-incorporated entity under the name Vegam Solutions Pte Ltd, while continuing engineering and delivery operations in India. [5]

During this period, the company developed the Vegam SFS manufacturing operations platform and related systems for industrial monitoring, maintenance management, production scheduling, and manufacturing traceability. [1] [2]

Trade-publication coverage during the late 2010s and early 2020s described the company's work in relation to industrial IoT, manufacturing operations management, and smart-factory systems. [1] [2]

By 2019, the company reported deployments across more than 100 manufacturing plants internationally. [17]

Corporate restructuring and AI expansion (2024–2026)

In 2024, a related Indian operating entity, Vegam Smart Factory Solutions Private Limited, was established in Bengaluru as part of the group's manufacturing-software and engineering operations.

In 2025, the company introduced AI Intime, an enterprise artificial-intelligence platform focused on manufacturing and industrial operations environments. [15]

By 2025, the company reported deployments across more than 300 manufacturing plants in approximately 60 countries. [17]

Products [edit]

Edit

AI Intime

AI Intime is an enterprise artificial-intelligence platform developed by Vegam Solutions for manufacturing and industrial operations environments. The platform supports role-specific AI agents designed for operational workflows, industrial data access, and enterprise knowledge retrieval. [15]

The platform has been used in manufacturing-related use cases including analytical laboratory report analysis, operational KPI retrieval, customer-support workflows, industrial training systems, and voice-enabled shop-floor assistance. [15]

According to company materials, AI Intime supports integrations with enterprise systems including manufacturing execution systems (MES), enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems, laboratory information-management systems (LIMS), and industrial operational databases. [15]

AI Intime is an enterprise agentic AI platform developed by Vegam Solutions for deployment in on-premises and air-gapped environments.[citation needed] The product is positioned for organisations operating in regulated environments, including manufacturing, financial services, healthcare, defence, and research.[citation needed]

Vegam Robotics

Edit

Vegam Robotics is the robotics and industrial-automation division of Vegam Solutions focused on autonomous material movement, industrial sampling systems, and factory automation technologies. The division develops robotics systems intended for manufacturing and industrial operations environments. [16]

Products developed under the Vegam Robotics initiative have included AutoDock, an autonomous docking and charging system for industrial vehicles; SampleRun, an automated industrial sample-handling system; and autonomous mobile robots (AMRs) designed for material movement and logistics workflows within manufacturing facilities. [16]

According to company materials, the robotics platform has been designed for use in industrial environments including manufacturing plants, warehouses, and process-industry operations. [16]

Vegam SFS

Edit

Vegam SFS is a manufacturing operations management (MOM) and manufacturing execution system (MES) platform developed by Vegam Solutions for process and discrete manufacturing industries. The platform is used for production management, industrial traceability, material movement, quality operations, and plant-level manufacturing execution. [1]

Development of the platform began during the company's early manufacturing digitisation projects in the late 2000s, including an initial deployment associated with a Henkel manufacturing facility in Australia in 2007. The platform has since been deployed across more than 300 manufacturing plants in approximately 60 countries. [1]

Vegam SFS integrates with enterprise and industrial systems including enterprise resource planning (ERP) platforms, distributed control systems (DCS), programmable logic controllers (PLC), supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA) systems, laboratory information-management systems (LIMS), and industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) infrastructure. [1]

The platform supports paperless manufacturing operations through digital workflows, electronic batch records, digital standard operating procedures (SOPs), production monitoring, and plant-wide operational traceability. [1]

Modules

Vegam SFS includes modules related to goods receipt (GR), warehouse management, staging operations, manufacturing execution, dispatch and logistics workflows, laboratory and quality management, production scheduling, industrial labelling, predictive maintenance, and operational-efficiency monitoring. [1]

  • Goods receipt (GR) – manages inbound material handling, unloading validation, weighbridge integration, and inventory registration.
  • Warehouse management and staging – supports warehouse visibility, inventory tracking, material staging, pre-weighing, and production-line material movement.
  • Manufacturing execution – manages production workflows, digital SOPs, process traceability, operator guidance, and production monitoring.
  • Dispatch and logistics – supports outbound logistics, shipment preparation, packaging validation, material traceability, and dispatch documentation.
  • LIMS and quality management – integrates laboratory operations, quality checkpoints, certificate-of-analysis workflows, and testing processes.
  • Production scheduling – supports production planning, sequencing, resource allocation, and manufacturing coordination.
  • Predictive maintenance – includes maintenance scheduling, equipment-health monitoring, downtime analysis, and industrial condition-monitoring workflows.
  • Industrial labelling – manages production and regulatory labelling workflows, including GHS-compliant labels.
  • Industrial integration – supports integration with ERP systems, DCS/PLC infrastructure, SCADA platforms, SAP environments, and industrial IoT systems.

Legacy products

Prior to the expansion of the Vegam SFS platform, the company developed several software products related to manufacturing operations, industrial monitoring, maintenance management, and production scheduling.

Earlier products that were later retired or consolidated into broader manufacturing operations offerings included SFS Lite; vCMS, a condition-monitoring system for industrial equipment; Vegam 4i; Vegam Label; vMaint4, a maintenance-management platform supporting preventive and predictive maintenance workflows; vOEE, an overall equipment effectiveness (OEE) monitoring system; vProMax; and Zing.[2][3][4]

Several earlier products were later consolidated into broader manufacturing operations and industrial automation offerings developed by the company.

Corporate operations [edit]

Vegam Solutions traces its origins to Aureole Technologies Private Limited, established in Bengaluru, India, in 2000. [5] The company's operations have subsequently been conducted through entities in multiple jurisdictions, including Singapore-based Vegam Solutions Pte Ltd, United States-based Vegam Inc, UAE-based Vegam FZCO, and the India-based engineering entity Vegam Smart Factory Solutions Private Limited.

The company's principal engineering, software-development, and delivery operations are based in India.

Vegam Solutions develops software and industrial-automation systems for manufacturing environments in sectors including chemicals, coatings, construction materials, metals, and industrial manufacturing. [1] [2]

According to company materials, the company's manufacturing-operations platforms have been deployed across more than 300 manufacturing plants in approximately 60 countries. [17]

Corporate affairs [edit]

Leadership

Subramanyam Kasibhat is the chief executive officer and co-founder of Vegam Solutions. Prior to founding the company, he worked at Texas Instruments India in engineering and business-development roles. [11]

Savita Kasibhat is the chief technology officer and co-founder of the company and has been associated with its engineering and technology-development activities since its founding. [11]

Other senior leadership associated with the company has included Hemang Shah, Vice President and Head of IIoT Products and Solutions Management, and Anant Kolhar, Vice President of Strategy and Operations Management. [11]

Ownership and funding

Vegam is privately held and has remained bootstrapped, without external venture funding.[13]
